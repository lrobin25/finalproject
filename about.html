BYLAWS of the WOMEN’S GOLF ASSOCIATION

Article I – Name

The name of this Organization shall be the Women’s Golf Association.

Article II – Object

The object of this Association is the promotion of good fellowship and sportsmanship among its members, and to encourage the active participation of its members in playing golf and taking part in golf tournaments conducted in accordance with rules of the USGA except as modified by local rules of the course.

Article III – Meetings

Section I Annual Meetings
Official Annual Business Meetings are set for each year as follows: Annual Spring Golf Meeting – Opening of official tournament season. Annual Fall Golf Meeting – Nomination of officers and presentation of awards.
Section II Place

The meetings shall be held in the clubhouse of Hiwan Golf Club, unless otherwise specified by the Governing Board.

Section III Special Meetings
Special meetings shall be called at any time by the order of the President or any (2) members of the Governing Board to the Secretary who shall give notice of special meetings at least seven (7) days before the date of the meetings by posting such notice on the bulletin board in the women’s locker room.

Article IV – Membership and Dues Section I Eligibility

Section 1
A. Membership in this Association shall be open to female dues paying members and females associated with dues paying members of Hiwan Golf Club and to widows of former resident members of Hiwan Golf Club who retain dues paying membership status. Junior Girl golfers fifteen (15) years or
Section 2 Membership
  • All women shall be eligible to play in the Women’s Golf Association if they have a handicap not exceeding twenty (20).
  • They may not, however, participate in the Club Championship Tournament.
  • A Maximum Hiwan Handicap of 44 is required to be eligible for membership in the eighteen hole group.
  • Once a member, there is no maximum Hiwan handicap.
  • There is no maximum Handicap Index required for the nine hole group.
  • For all Intra-club play, a maximum Hiwan Handicap of 44 is required in the eighteen hole group, expect those who joined prior to October, 1989.
On Guest Days:
  • To be eligible for prizes, a guest must be a non-Hiwan member.
  • If a guest does not have a conforming handicap, we would hope a true handicap of her ability would be given.
  • The maximum handicap for a guest will be 36, or one will be assigned at the discretion of the Guest Day Committee, or the Pro Shop.
Membership Groups

Members will belong to one of two groups within the Association, the 18 Hole Group or the 9 Hole Group.

Section 3 Dues, Period Payable
Dues shall become due at the annual Spring Golf Meeting. Any member whose dues have not been paid shall be ineligible to participate in any tournament until dues are paid.
Section 4 Dues, Amount
Dues shall be decided upon by the Governing Board.

Article V – Governing Body

Section 1
Officers The business and affairs of the Association shall be managed by a Governing Board consisting of: President (18-Hole and 9 Hole), Vice President (18 Hole and 9 Hole), Secretary (18 Hole), Treasurer (18 Hole), Tournament Chair, Handicap Chair and President of the previous year. The elected officers shall serve for a term of one (1) year beginning on January 1st, or until their successors are elected and assume office.
Section 2

Nominations shall be solicited by a Nominating Committee consisting of the President and current Vice President. The nominees’ names shall be posted on the bulletin board in the Women’s locker room before the Annual Fall Golf Meeting. The presiding officer at the Annual Fall Golf Meeting shall ask for nominations from the floor. All nominations shall be affirmed at this time. If for any reason a new President is not elected or a new President is unable to assume her duties, then the responsibility for the selection of a new President falls on the Governing Board. Governing Board Meetings.


The Governing Board shall meet before the Annual Spring Golf Meeting, and/or at anytime the President deems necessary to transact business for the Association. Quorum. At all meetings of the Governing Board, five (5) members shall constitute a quorum. Duties of Officers


The Officers of the Association shall respectively exercise and perform their respective powers, duties, and functions as stated below, and as may be assigned to them by the Association.

A. President.
The President shall preside at all meetings of the Association and of the Governing Board and shall have general supervision of all affairs of the Association. She shall supervise all committee activities, an ex officio member of all committees and shall be notified of all meetings. She shall appoint all standing committee chairs and committee members.

The Secretary shall keep accurate minutes of all meetings of the Association and the Governing Board. She shall keep a register of the members of the Association and shall be responsible for the giving of notice of meetings. Article VI – Non Elective Offices

The Governing Board may appoint such other Committees as deemed necessary.
Section 1 Standing Committees
The Standing Committees shall consist of the following: Handicap, Rules, Tournaments, Awards, Publicity, Guest Day. They shall be guided by the Golf Committee Manual and the USGA Rules of Golf, as they relate to their duties. They shall perform all duties incident to their chairwomanship and such duties as assigned by the Association President.
A. The Rules Committee
shall keep the membership informed of USGA and Local Rules.
B. The Handicap Committee
shall be comprised of the Handicap Chair, Rules Chair, President, Vice President and Tournament Chair.
Section 2 Budget CommitteesH
A. The 18 Hole Budget Committee shall consist of the President, Vice President, Treasurer, Awards Chair, and Tournament Chair. B. The 9 Hole Budget Committee shall consist of the President, Vice President, and Treasurer.

Article VII – Representatives

Section 1 Team Captains
The A and B Team shall select a Team Captain by any method of their choice. Teams will consist, as far as possible, of those HWGA members with the lowest established handicaps. The Team Captains have the discretion, however, to select team members based on the following eligibility standards: A. Participation in ten (10) days of the weekly HWGA play days.
B. Agreement to post ALL 9 and 18 hole scores played at home and away.
C. Willingness to play team matches scheduled both at home and away.
D. Extenuating circumstances may dictate exceptions to these standards.
Section 2 State Representatives
The Representation to the Colorado Women’s Golf Association are the President and a designated person. They shall attend the Annual Spring Meeting of the Association as delegates from Hiwan Women’s Golf Association. They have the two official votes on behalf of Hiwan Women’s Golf Association at the Association Meetings. In the event she is unable to attend, the President shall appoint someone in her place. She shall advise the Association of State Directives.

Article VIII – Amendments

These Bylaws may be repealed or amended, or new Bylaws adopted, by a two-thirds vote of members present at any regular meeting of the membership.

Article IX – Authority on Procedure

Robert’s Rule of Order Revised shall be the final authority as to parliamentary procedure at all meetings of the members, insofar as they do not conflict with any provisions of these Bylaws.
